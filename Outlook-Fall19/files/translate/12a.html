The Gateway to Good Eating
UC Davis students and the Davis community benefit from an abundance
of fresh organic fruits, vegetables and flowers grown at the Student Farm.

Many students like recent graduate Katie Blackwood (pictured, SAFS ’18)
participate in the Community Supported Agriculture program that provides
weekly baskets to subscribers. Throughout the year, students also glean
tomatoes, peppers, squash, sweet corn and other produce items and
make them available at no cost to students at a number of campus outlets,
including the ASUCD Pantry and Fruit and Veggie Up! located in the
Memorial Union. Other distribution sites include resource centers that
support first generation students and UC Davis’ diverse student body.

UC Davis Dining Services also purchases Student Farm produce to ensure
greater access to fresh, healthy food in the dining commons. To learn more
about the Student Farm’s food security efforts, please visit
caes.ucdavis.edu/news/food. HECTOR AMEZCUA/UC Davis
